1 !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
2 ! Variables that have to do with equilibrium quantities and the grid used in !
3 ! the calculations: !
4 ! - The equilibrium variables are comprised of the variables that !
5 ! result from the equilibrium calculation, such as pressure, rotational !
6 ! transform, etc. !
7 ! - The flux variables are tabulated on a 1D grid. !
8 ! - The metric variables are tabulated on a 3D grid !
9 ! + In the normal coordinate, they are tabulated in the equilibrium !
10 ! grid. !
11 ! + In the angular coordinates, they are tabulated in the solution !
12 ! grid (VMEC), or in the equilibrium grid followed by an adaptation !
13 ! to the solution grid (HELENA). !
14 ! - However, this is not necessarily always the case, as it depends on !
15 ! the grid angles being aligned with the grid (e.g. Providing theta and !
16 ! zeta in the equilibrium grid so that the magnetic field lines are !
17 ! followed. !
18 ! Note: In general in PB3D, there are two kinds of variables, differing from !
19 ! one another in the way in which they are tabulated: !
20 ! - variables tabulated on the full output grid of the equilibrium code !
21 ! - variables tabulated in an internal grid of this code !
22 ! In many places in the code a range in the normal coordinate is selected !
23 ! for each of the variables on different processes. This selection has to be !
24 ! done correctly and things can get a little bit complicated if trimmed !
25 ! grids are used (grids that have no overlap between processes). See !
26 ! grid_ops. !
27 !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!

77  ! metric equilibrium type
78  ! The arrays here are of the form
79  ! - (angle_1,angle_2,r) for scalar vars. without derivs.
80  ! - (angle_1,angle_2,r,D123) for scalar vars. with derivs.
81  ! - (angle_1,angle_2,r,6/9,D1,D2,D3) for tensorial vars. with derivs.
82  ! where it is refered to the discussion of the grid type for an explanation
83  ! of the angles angle_1 and angle_2.
84  ! The last index refers to the derivatives in coordinate 1, 2 and 3, which
85  ! refer to the coordinates described in [ADD REF]:
86  ! - For E(quilibrium) coordinates, they are (r,theta,zeta)_E
87  ! - For F(lux) coordinates, they are (alpha,psi,ang_par)_F, where
88  ! + alpha = zeta - q theta and ang_par = theta for pol. flux,
89  ! + alpha = -theta + iota zeta and ang_par = zeta for tor. flux.
90  ! The fourth index for tensorial variables correspond to the 9 or 6
91  ! (symmetric) different values:
92  ! (1 4 7) (1 )
93  ! (2 5 8) or (2 4 )
94  ! (3 6 9) (3 5 6)
95  ! Note that Fortran only allows for 7 dimensions in arrays.
